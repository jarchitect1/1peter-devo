## 1. The setting

| | |
|---|---|
| **Author** | The apostle **Peter** (1:1), written through Silvanus (Silas) as amanuensis (5:12). Universally received in the early church; Petrine authorship is the standard evangelical position. |
| **Written to** | The "**elect exiles of the Dispersion**" in Pontus, Galatia, Cappadocia, Asia, and Bithynia (1:1), largely Gentile believers across northern Asia Minor. |
| **From** | "Babylon" (5:13), almost certainly a symbolic name for **Rome**, ~AD 62-64, before the Neronian persecution reached its peak. |
| **Occasion** | Believers facing social hostility, slander, and trial "for the name." Peter steadies their hope, calls them to holy living, and teaches them to suffer well after Christ's pattern. |
| **Key metaphor** | **Exiles and sojourners** (1:1, 17; 2:11): citizens of heaven living as a colony of grace in a watching world. |
| **Mood** | Tender and urgent, doxological and pastoral, written by a shepherd who knows suffering and failure firsthand. |

## 2. The map: five chapters

| Chapter | Center of gravity | Key verses |
|---|---|---|
| Ch. 1<br/><span class="small">A living hope for exiles</span> | Born again to a living hope through Christ's resurrection; be holy as God is holy. Love one another earnestly, born of imperishable seed. | 1:3-4, 1:15-16, 1:22-23 |
| Ch. 2<br/><span class="small">A royal priesthood</span> | Living stones built into a spiritual house, a chosen race and **royal priesthood**. As sojourners, live such good lives that the slander is silenced; Christ left us an example. | 2:5, 2:9-10, 2:11-12, 2:24 |
| Ch. 3<br/><span class="small">Witness in the closest relationships</span> | Husbands and wives as heirs of grace; unity and blessing in the fellowship; suffering for righteousness, with Christ's victory proclaimed. | 3:7, 3:8-9, 3:14-15, 3:18 |
| Ch. 4<br/><span class="small">Living for God in the fiery trial</span> | Arm with Christ's mindset; love and serve with the gifts received; do not be surprised at the fiery trial, but glorify God in it and entrust your soul to a faithful Creator. | 4:7-10, 4:12-13, 4:19 |
| Ch. 5<br/><span class="small">Shepherd, humble, watch</span> | Elders shepherd willingly and eagerly, as examples; all clothe yourselves with humility; cast anxiety on Him; resist the devil, firm in the faith. | 5:2-3, 5:6-7, 5:8-9, 5:10 |

## 3. One sentence summary

<KeyIdea label="The letter in a breath">
Because God has given us a living hope in the risen Christ and made us His chosen, holy priesthood, we live as faithful sojourners, holy in conduct, submissive in our stations, joyful in suffering, humble in the fellowship, and watchful to the end.
</KeyIdea>

## 4. The threads running through all five chapters

1. **Identity before conduct**: who we are (elect exiles, born again, a royal priesthood) grounds what we do.
2. **Hope secured by the resurrection**: an imperishable inheritance kept in heaven, living and active now (1:3-5).
3. **Holiness as God's people**: "be holy, for I am holy" (1:15-16), lived out in the world.
4. **Sojourners and exiles**: at home in heaven, resident but not at home on earth (1:1, 17; 2:11).
5. **Suffering after Christ**: unjust suffering borne patiently after the Shepherd's pattern, with a blessing mindset (2:18-25; 3:13-22; 4:12-19).
6. **Submission in God's order**: to authority, masters, within marriage, and within the flock (2:13-3:7; 5:5).
7. **Grace, humility, watchfulness**: the posture of the priesthood to the end (5:5-11).

<Aside title="The series through-line" type="note">
**The priesthood of all believers** (2:9). Every lesson traces back to this: a holy people offering spiritual sacrifices, proclaiming God's excellencies, shepherding and serving one another, until suffering gives way to glory.
</Aside>
