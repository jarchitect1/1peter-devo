<p class="lead">A short glossary. Once these words are in your bones, the letter reads as one sustained argument: a holy priesthood living out a living hope.</p>

<dl class="glossary">

<dt>Parepidēmos / paroikos <span class="grk">παρεπίδημος / πάροικος</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>Sojourner, exile, resident alien.</em> Peter's signature name for believers: not tourists, but residents who do not belong. Earth is not home; heaven is. The posture shapes holiness, hope, and how we bear slander.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 1:1, 17; 2:11.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Elpis <span class="grk">ἐλπίς</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>Hope</em>, but not wishful thinking, a confident, living expectation anchored in Christ's resurrection and an inheritance kept in heaven. "Set your hope fully on the grace" (1:13).
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 1:3, 13, 21; 3:15.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Anagennaō / anagennēsis <span class="grk">ἀναγεννάω</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>To beget again, cause to be born again.</em> The new birth, through the living and enduring word of God, "into a living hope" (1:3). Imperishable seed, imperishable life.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 1:3, 23.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Ekteneōs <span class="grk">ἐκτενῶς</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>Earnestly, strenuously, at full stretch.</em> The kind of love Peter commands: not polite affection but a love that strains and reaches, that "covers a multitude of sins" (4:8). The same word is used of the Lord's earnest prayer in Luke 22:44. The Church's love under trial is to have this stretched, unflagging quality.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 1:22; 4:8.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Hagios <span class="grk">ἅγιος</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>Holy, set apart.</em> "As he who called you is holy, be holy in all your conduct, since it is written, 'You shall be holy, for I am holy.'" Holiness is the family likeness of God's people.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 1:15-16; 2:5, 9.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Basileion hierateuma <span class="grk">βασίλειον ἱεράτευμα</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>Royal priesthood.</em> The series' through-line. Every believer is a priest: access to God directly through Christ, and a calling to offer spiritual sacrifices and proclaim God's excellencies. No separate priestly caste.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 2:5, 9.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Laos eis peripoiēsin <span class="grk">λαὸς εἰς περιποίησιν</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>A people for God's own possession.</em> Drawn from Ex 19:5-6 and applied now to the church, Jew and Gentile together. God's treasured possession, formed to display His glory.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 2:9-10.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Oikodomeō / oikos pneumatikos <span class="grk">οἰκοδομέω / οἶκος πνευματικός</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>To build / a spiritual house.</em> Christ the living cornerstone, believers the living stones built into a temple where God dwells by His Spirit. Corporate before it is individual.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 2:4-5.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Paschō / pathēma <span class="grk">πάσχω / πάθημα</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>To suffer / suffering.</em> Not a detour but a calling: believers are "called" to it because Christ suffered for them, leaving an example (2:21). Suffering tests, refines, and participates in Christ's own path to glory.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 2:19-21; 3:14, 17; 4:1, 12-13, 15-16, 19; 5:9-10.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Hypotassō <span class="grk">ὑποτάσσω</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>To submit, order oneself under.</em> A voluntary placing of oneself within God's appointed structures (civil authority, masters, marriage, the flock), as unto the Lord. Submission is not inferiority; it is trust in God's sovereignty.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 2:13, 18; 3:1, 5; 5:5.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Anastrephō / anastrophē <span class="grk">ἀναστρέφω / ἀναστροφή</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>To conduct oneself / way of life.</em> Peter's recurring call for a <em>visible</em> holiness: a "good way of life" that silences ignorance and commends the gospel.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 1:15, 18; 2:12; 3:1-2, 16.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Tapheinophrosynē / tapeinoō <span class="grk">ταπεινοφροσύνη / ταπεινόω</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>Humility of mind / to humble oneself.</em> "Clothe yourselves, all of you, with humility toward one another" (5:5). The garment of the priesthood, under the mighty hand of God.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 3:8; 5:5-6.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Poimainō / poimnion <span class="grk">ποιμαίνω / ποίμνιον</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>To shepherd / flock.</em> Elders shepherd the flock of God willingly, eagerly, by example, under the Chief Shepherd who will appear. A model of servant leadership for the assembly.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 5:2-4.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Grēgoreō / nēphō <span class="grk">γρηγορέω / νήφω</span></dt>
<dd>
  <em>Be watchful / be sober-minded.</em> The end-time posture of the priesthood: alert against the prowling devil, self-controlled in hope, resisting him firm in the faith.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 1:13; 4:7; 5:8.</span>
</dd>

<dt>Silvanus <span class="grk">Σιλουανός</span></dt>
<dd>
  Silas, Peter's faithful co-worker and the letter's amanuensis (5:12), the same Silas who served with Paul (Acts 15-18). A quiet bridge between the Petrine and Pauline circles.
  <span class="ref">Key text: 1 Pet 5:12.</span>
</dd>

</dl>
